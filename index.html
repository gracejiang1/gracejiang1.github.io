More nonsensical rambling to come below. Currently very impressed with how 3D printing for the first time turned out! I ended up creating the cageCube with 6 holes from scratch in fusion 360 bc the solidworks files I was provided with had a circle hole off-center (was not aligned with the square face's center). It didn't need to be perfect but I wanted to see if fusion could export the file to makerbot smoothly and all that jazz. Anyways, it turns out the microscope only requires the cube to have one hole. So I experimented with number and shapes of holes to try and find a cube with the lowest print time and few supports. Turns out a 3x3x3" cube with 2.5" diameter circular holes all around had a 4 hr print time, the lowest I could get it to without overcomplicating things and taking out too much material/decreasing stability. Unfortunately it does use 8 supports, but it turned out much better than expected. I also toyed with the idea of vertical rectangular holes just thin enough to not require supports but that's kind of pointless overall. Maybe I'll upload some diagrams of the other prototypes later. So right now it's sitting on my desk with raft and supports still attached. It actually looks sturdy with supports, so I might keep them there. I'll probably x-acto off the raft when I have time though. It blocks a bit of the bottom circle hole. To conclude, the makerbots are really convenient to use and I'm satisfied with the print quality. So far everything seems precise and the corners and edges of this cube are so sharp! I was considering getting clearance to use the Form 2 printer for this because of the support concerns and whatnot but there was no need for that in the end! The same project on the Form 2 would take over 7 hours.
